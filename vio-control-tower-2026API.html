V6 Brand Guardrails — Active in Every Entry

The Standard.
Non-Negotiable.

Every deliverable in this system is cross-referenced here. A Never List flag requires revision before any asset moves forward. This is not a checklist — it is the brand operating as intended.

Category Violations — Highest Severity
⚑ Anti-Aging Language — Hard no. No context makes it acceptable. The replacement is the philosophy: ages and stages, being in love with who you are now, always becoming. It is not a phrase swap. It is a way of thinking.
⚑ Defining VIO by what it is not — No contrast framing. No "finally." No "unlike other med spas." VIO is already the thing. State what it is.
⚑ "Protocol" in any context — Absolute no. Always a more specific, human word. "Your plan." "How to maintain between visits." Never "protocol."
⚑ Treatment packages / deals — A plan is built for someone. A package is sold to anyone. ClubVIO gives preferred rates — never discounts.
⚑ "Quiet luxury" to or for clients — Ralph Lauren does not explain its aesthetic. Create the standard. Do not name it.
⚑ Before-and-after as primary brand narrative — VIO uses before-and-afters. UGC can lead with the reveal. VIO campaigns do not. The story is ongoing becoming.
Language Violations — High Severity
⚑ Exclamation points — Never in editorial, website, or consultation copy. Exception: ClubVIO membership confirmation only.
⚑ "Practice" to mean ritual or routine — VIO does not offer "wellness practices." Absolute no in all contexts.
⚑ "VIO Med Spa" in copy — "VIO Med Spa" lives in the logo and materials only. In writing and speech: VIO.
⚑ Urgency / scarcity as default tactic — "Hurry, filling up fast." "Limited spots." "Book now." These are wrong-register. Scarcity is real and stated as fact (150 Founding Members — real). Never as a tactic.
⚑ Patient or customer — Client. Member. Guest. Never patient (wrong clinical distance). Never customer (transaction language).
