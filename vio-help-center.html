Treatment Questions
Collagen Production
PRF EZ Gel4▾
What is PRF EZ Gel?▾

PRF EZ Gel is derived entirely from your own blood — processed in a centrifuge to produce a concentrated fibrin gel containing your own growth factors, platelets, and proteins. This gel is injected into targeted areas where the growth factors signal tissue to regenerate and produce collagen. No synthetic material enters your body.

What makes PRF EZ Gel different from PRP?▾

PRF EZ Gel is a fibrin matrix — a gel structure that releases growth factors slowly over time. PRP is a liquid that delivers growth factors as an immediate bolus. PRF's sustained release is thought to produce a more prolonged regenerative signal. PRF also contains a fibrin scaffold that supports tissue architecture.

What areas can PRF EZ Gel treat?▾

PRF EZ Gel is used in the under-eye hollow, fine surface lines, temples, and the scalp for hair restoration. In the under-eye area specifically, its autologous nature eliminates the Tyndall effect risk that can occur with synthetic HA fillers placed superficially.

How soon do PRF EZ Gel results appear?▾

Results develop over four to eight weeks as growth factors stimulate tissue regeneration and collagen production. A series of three sessions spaced four to six weeks apart is typically recommended. The improvement is gradual — it looks like your skin performing better.

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP)2▾
What is PRP and how is it different from PRF?▾

Both are autologous, growth factor-based treatments derived from your own blood. PRP is a liquid platelet concentrate delivering growth factors as an immediate bolus. PRF EZ Gel is a fibrin matrix releasing them slowly over time. For skin and under-eye applications, PRF's slower release may sustain the regenerative signal longer.

What is PRP used for at VIO?▾

PRP is used as a standalone injectable for under-eye hollows and fine surface lines, topically during microneedling to amplify collagen induction, and in scalp treatment for hair restoration.

Radiesse®4▾
What is Radiesse and how does it work?▾

Radiesse is a calcium hydroxylapatite filler — not hyaluronic acid. CaHA microspheres provide immediate structural volume while signaling the body to produce its own collagen around them. Over months, the gel carrier absorbs and the CaHA gradually breaks down, leaving behind a collagen scaffold that sustains the improvement.

What is hyperdilute Radiesse?▾

Hyperdilute Radiesse is the same product at much lower concentration — diluted with saline and lidocaine and spread broadly across the skin. At this dilution it no longer adds volume but acts as a biostimulator, triggering diffuse collagen production and skin tightening across the face, neck, or body.

Can Radiesse be dissolved?▾

No. Radiesse is calcium hydroxylapatite — there is no equivalent of hyaluronidase for CaHA. It cannot be dissolved. The body absorbs it on its own timeline. This makes provider selection and dose decisions especially important.

How long does Radiesse last?▾

Structural Radiesse typically lasts twelve to eighteen months for immediate volume correction. Collagen stimulation can extend improvement to two years or more.

Sculptra®4▾
What is Sculptra and how is it different from filler?▾

Sculptra is poly-L-lactic acid — a collagen stimulator, not a filler. It prompts your body to produce its own collagen gradually over months. The results look like your face at its best because the volume created is genuinely your body's own tissue. The process takes time. The outcome reflects it.

How many sessions does Sculptra require?▾

Most clients require a series of two to three sessions, spaced four to six weeks apart. Results build over three to six months after the series. Sculptra is for someone building something lasting — not seeking immediate correction.

How long does Sculptra last?▾

Sculptra's collagen-building effects typically last two years or more. Some clinical data supports outcomes at four to five years. Because results are produced by your own biology, longevity reflects how your body maintains that collagen over time.

What should I expect after Sculptra injections?▾

You may look temporarily fuller immediately after due to the diluting solution — this is water, not Sculptra, and subsides within days. Actual results build over three to six months. You will be given a massage protocol for the first five days — this is important for even distribution.

Dermal Fillers
Belotero Balance (+)7▾
What is Belotero Balance (+)?▾

Belotero Balance (+) is a hyaluronic acid filler formulated with a distinctive cohesion profile that allows it to integrate directly into skin tissue rather than sitting beneath it. This makes it particularly well-suited for delicate areas where other fillers can look or feel heavy: vertical lip lines, under-eye hollows, and fine surface lines. At VIO, it is selected when the precision of the placement demands a product with high cohesion and natural flex.

What makes Belotero different from other HA fillers?▾

Belotero Balance (+) is manufactured for high cohesion, low viscosity, and low elasticity — which means it spreads more softly and integrates more seamlessly into skin tissue than thicker fillers. In areas of significant facial movement, or in the delicate under-eye and perioral zones, this translates to outcomes that look and feel natural without compromising your expression.

How soon do Belotero results appear?▾

Belotero Balance (+) smooths lines and hollows immediately upon injection. The full outcome settles as any minor swelling resolves — typically within a few days. Results in the treated areas last six months or more, depending on the area and individual factors.

Is Belotero a good option for first-time filler clients?▾

Belotero Balance (+)'s soft, integrating formula makes it well-suited for first-time clients who want a subtle, natural outcome — particularly in fine-line or under-eye areas where a heavier product would be inappropriate. Your VIO consultation is where that determination is made. We consider your anatomy, your goals, and the right product for the right area — not the other way around.

What are the potential side effects of Belotero Balance (+)?▾

Common side effects include temporary swelling, redness, bruising, tenderness, or itching at the injection site. These typically resolve within seven days. Belotero Balance (+) is FDA-approved and has been evaluated in clinical studies for safety. As with all injectable treatments, there are rare but serious risks associated with vascular injection — which is why provider selection and clinical training matter.

Can Belotero be combined with other treatments?▾

Yes. Belotero Balance (+) is frequently used alongside other filler products or neuromodulators as part of a broader facial plan. It complements collagen-stimulating fillers well — addressing surface lines while a regenerative product addresses deeper structural volume. Your VIO provider will design a plan that considers each product's role in the overall outcome.

How many syringes of Belotero will I need?▾

That depends entirely on your facial anatomy, the areas being addressed, and the degree of correction you are looking for. There is no standard answer — and at VIO, we do not start from a quantity. We start from your face. Your provider will give you a realistic recommendation during your consultation, and you will understand exactly what is being placed, where, and why.

Dermal Fillers7▾
What are dermal fillers?▾

Dermal fillers are injectable treatments that restore volume, smooth lines, and refine facial contours. Most are made of hyaluronic acid — a substance that occurs naturally in the skin — and are absorbed by the body over time. At VIO, fillers are part of a considered aesthetic plan, not a standalone procedure. Your provider maps your facial structure, discusses your goals, and places filler with the precision of someone who understands both the science and the artistry of the face.

How long do dermal fillers last?▾

Most hyaluronic acid fillers last between six months and two years, depending on the product, the area treated, and how your body metabolizes the filler. Areas with more movement — like lips and perioral lines — tend to metabolize filler more quickly. Your VIO provider will give you a realistic timeline and a plan for maintaining your outcome over time.

Will filler make me look different — or unnatural?▾

The right filler, placed correctly, should make you look like yourself — rested, defined, and at ease. What you notice when filler is done well is not the filler. It is the version of your face that was always there. At VIO, the conversation about natural-looking outcomes begins at your consultation and is part of every decision we make together.

Is there downtime after dermal fillers?▾

Most people return to their regular day immediately after filler treatment. You may notice some swelling, redness, or bruising at the injection sites for a few days — this is normal and typically resolves within a week. For the first 24 hours, avoid strenuous activity, prolonged sun or heat exposure, aspirin or NSAIDs, and alcohol. Your provider will walk you through everything before you leave.

Do dermal fillers hurt?▾

Most people describe the sensation as mild pressure — not pain. Your VIO provider applies topical numbing prior to treatment, and many filler formulations contain lidocaine, which further reduces discomfort during the injection. The experience is brief. Most treatments are complete in under thirty minutes.

How are dermal fillers different from Botox or neuromodulators?▾

Fillers and neuromodulators do different things, and at VIO, they are often part of the same considered plan. Fillers restore volume and smooth lines that exist at rest — structural work. Neuromodulators like Botox and Dysport address dynamic movement — softening the expression lines that appear when you animate. Your provider will explain where each plays a role in your specific facial architecture.

What areas can dermal fillers treat?▾

At VIO, fillers are placed with intention across a range of areas: smile lines, under-eye hollows, lip lines and volume, chin definition, cheek structure, and perioral lines. Each area has its own clinical considerations. Your provider maps the face as a whole — not as isolated problems — and places filler where it serves the overall balance.

Juvéderm® Collection4▾
What is the Juvéderm collection?▾

Juvéderm is a family of hyaluronic acid fillers — each engineered for a different area and depth of placement. Voluma provides structural lift in the cheeks and midface. Volbella refines lips and fine perioral lines. Vollure addresses deeper folds with natural movement. Your provider selects the specific product that serves your anatomy.

How long does Juvéderm last?▾

Duration varies by product. Voluma is FDA-approved with two-year longevity data. Volbella and Vollure typically last twelve to eighteen months. Juvéderm Ultra in the lips around twelve months. Areas with more movement tend to metabolize filler more quickly.

Can Juvéderm be dissolved?▾

Yes. Juvéderm is a hyaluronic acid filler, which can be dissolved with hyaluronidase if needed — an enzyme that breaks down HA. VIO providers are trained in hyaluronidase protocol. This reversibility is one of the key clinical advantages of HA fillers.

Is Juvéderm right for lips?▾

Juvéderm Volbella and Ultra are among the most established options for lip treatment — both FDA-approved and well-studied. Whether they are right for you depends on your goal: subtle definition, more volume, or perioral line correction each call for a different approach. Your provider maps the lip as a whole before placing anything.

RHA® Collection3▾
What is the RHA Collection?▾

RHA stands for Resilient Hyaluronic Acid. Manufactured using a preservation-synthesis process that produces HA chains structurally closer to your skin's own HA, the result is a gel that integrates into dynamic tissue and deforms with expression rather than resisting it. RHA is the only filler collection specifically engineered for dynamic facial zones.

Why would my provider choose RHA over other fillers?▾

RHA is selected when movement compatibility matters most — smile lines, marionette lines, and perioral areas see significant mechanical stress from facial animation. Standard fillers can feel rigid under movement in these zones. RHA flexes with the face.

How long does RHA last?▾

RHA is FDA-approved with up to fifteen months of duration in dynamic areas — longer than many HA fillers in the same zones, likely because its movement compatibility reduces the mechanical breakdown that accelerates filler metabolism.

Restylane® Collection3▾
What is the Restylane collection?▾

Restylane is a family of hyaluronic acid fillers. Products using OBT technology — Kysse, Defyne, and Refyne — flex with facial movement, making them well-suited for dynamic areas like lips and smile lines. Restylane Lyft provides structural support in the cheeks. Each product has a specific movement and integration profile.

What makes Restylane Kysse different for lips?▾

Kysse uses OBT technology producing a flexible gel that deforms with lip movement rather than resisting it. The result is lip augmentation that feels and moves naturally. For clients who have experienced stiffness from other lip fillers, Kysse is worth considering.

How long does Restylane last?▾

Most Restylane products last up to twelve months depending on the area and individual metabolism.

Lift & Contour
Kybella®4▾
What is Kybella and how does it work?▾

Kybella is deoxycholic acid — a molecule your body already produces to break down dietary fat. When injected into the fat beneath the chin, it permanently destroys the cell membranes of those fat cells. The body eliminates the destroyed cells through its natural processes. Once gone, those cells do not return.

How many Kybella sessions are needed?▾

Most clients require two to four sessions, spaced about a month apart. Results accumulate with each treatment as additional fat cells are destroyed. Your provider assesses after each session before recommending the next.

What is the downtime after Kybella?▾

Significant swelling, bruising, and numbness in the under-chin area are expected after each session and can last one to two weeks. This is the treatment working — plan your schedule accordingly.

Is Kybella permanent?▾

Yes. The fat cells destroyed by Kybella do not regenerate. Once an adequate series is complete, the reduction in submental fullness is lasting. Significant weight gain can increase remaining fat cells, but the destroyed ones will not return.

PDO Threading4▾
What is PDO threading?▾

PDO threading uses fine absorbable sutures placed under the skin to mechanically lift and reposition tissue. The threads work two ways: an immediate mechanical lift, and a progressive collagen response as the threads dissolve over four to six months. The threads are gone, but the structural improvement lasts considerably longer.

What areas can PDO threads treat?▾

At VIO, threading addresses the jawline, jowls, brow, mid-face, and neck — areas where filler alone cannot adequately address laxity. It physically repositions tissue rather than adding volume.

How long do PDO thread results last?▾

The threads dissolve in four to six months. The collagen scaffold they stimulate maintains improvement for twelve to eighteen months. Maintenance around the twelve-month mark is typically recommended.

Is PDO threading painful?▾

Performed under topical and local anesthetic — discomfort during treatment is minimal. Some clients experience soreness, mild bruising, and a sense of tightness for several days as tissue settles.

Wrinkle Relaxers
Botox®5▾
What is Botox and how does it work?▾

Botox is a purified form of botulinum toxin that temporarily relaxes the muscles responsible for creating expression lines. When injected, it interrupts the signal that tells those muscles to contract — softening the lines that form when you animate. It does not change your face. It softens the movement behind it.

How long does Botox last?▾

For most people, Botox lasts three to four months. As the effects gradually wear off, muscle movement returns and lines reappear. With consistent treatment over time, many clients find the lines become less pronounced — the muscle learns a softer default. Your VIO provider will help you establish a cadence that keeps your outcome where you want it.

Will Botox make me look frozen or expressionless?▾

Not when it is placed correctly. The goal of neuromodulator treatment at VIO is to soften — not eliminate — movement. You should still be able to raise your brows, squint, smile, and show every expression that matters to you. Frozen faces are the result of over-treatment, not the treatment itself. Your provider maps your movement before placing anything.

The Tri-Immune injection combines Glutathione, Vitamin C, and Zinc intramuscularly — three nutrients with distinct and complementary roles in immune function. Addresses multiple immune pathways in a single injection at concentrations that exceed what oral supplementation achieves.

When is the Tri-Immune injection most useful?▾

Proactively before high-exposure seasons, immediately after known illness exposure, during recovery to rebuild immune resilience, or as part of an ongoing protocol for clients whose immune function is chronically stressed.

Peptide Therapy
GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)2▾
What is GHK-Cu?▾

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper-binding peptide that declines with age. It stimulates collagen and elastin synthesis, promotes tissue repair, supports hair follicle health, and has broad anti-inflammatory properties. One of the most researched and lowest-risk peptides in regenerative medicine.

What is GHK-Cu used for at VIO?▾

Used in scalp treatment protocols supporting follicle health alongside PRF, and as a systemic regenerative support peptide for skin collagen and tissue repair. Also used topically as an adjunct to microneedling.

Oxytocin2▾
What is oxytocin therapy?▾

Oxytocin is an endogenous neuropeptide naturally released during connection, touch, and safety. It mediates trust, emotional bonding, and stress regulation. Administered intranasally in a clinical wellness context, it supports stress resilience, emotional regulation, and intimacy.

Who benefits from oxytocin in a wellness plan?▾

Clients experiencing chronic stress, difficulty with emotional connection, anxiety not fully addressed through other approaches, or those seeking to support the intimacy dimension of their wellness picture. Pairs well with PT-141 in sexual wellness protocols.

PT-141 (Bremelanotide)2▾
What is PT-141 and how does it work?▾

PT-141 is a melanocortin receptor agonist — it acts on receptors in the brain's motivational and desire pathways rather than on sex hormones or blood flow. FDA-approved as Vyleesi for hypoactive sexual desire disorder in premenopausal women. Used off-label for men with sexual dysfunction.

Who is PT-141 appropriate for?▾

Women with hypoactive sexual desire disorder and men experiencing sexual dysfunction not addressed by other approaches. Works through the nervous system rather than through hormones or circulation — relevant when those are not the primary limiting factor.

Selank / Semax2▾
What are Selank and Semax?▾

Selank and Semax are nootropic peptides. Selank modulates GABA, serotonin, and dopamine systems to reduce anxiety while maintaining mental clarity — without sedation. Semax stimulates BDNF, the protein your brain uses to build and maintain neural connections, supporting memory and focus. Both administered intranasally.

Who are Selank and Semax appropriate for?▾

Clients in cognitively demanding environments seeking focus, clarity, and stress resilience without pharmaceutical intervention. Selank for the anxiety-focus intersection; Semax for memory and neuroplasticity.

Tesamorelin2▾
What is Tesamorelin?▾

Tesamorelin is a synthetic analogue of growth hormone-releasing hormone that signals the pituitary to produce growth hormone, which specifically targets visceral adipose tissue — the metabolically active deep abdominal fat around internal organs associated with cardiovascular risk. Unlike subcutaneous fat, visceral fat often does not respond to diet and exercise.

Who is Tesamorelin appropriate for?▾

Clients with elevated visceral fat — often identifiable by disproportionate abdominal girth relative to overall body composition. Also used alongside GLP-1 therapy for clients whose metabolic picture includes specific visceral fat reduction as a target.
